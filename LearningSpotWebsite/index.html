Unemployment remains an alarming issue globally. Rwanda in particular, unemployment remains a concerning issue where the study conducted in 2024Q1 shows that “approximately one person was unemployed for every eight in the labor force…. Gender disparities persist in unemployment, with females experiencing a higher rate at 14.5% compared to males at 11.5%... youth face a notably higher unemployment rate of 16.6% compared to adults at 10.3%. Urban areas also bear a heavier burden with an unemployment rate of 14% compared to rural areas at 12.3%.” reported by Tuyisenge, 2024. This unemployment is linked with gaps in skills mostly in digital skills. Moreover, a gap in English fluency in Rwanda is a key player. Recent research shows that only 4% of all teachers in Rwanda have attained the required fluency in 2024. In 2022, only 0.2% Rwandans spoke English fluently, while only 0.1% spoke French fluently.
